"Sit Up: L'Alça", my lastest product demonstration:

October 17, 2016 by Martí

Last couple of weeks, I've been working in a project that I'm very exited about.

"Sit Up: L'Alça" is a project that was started 10 years ago by Marta Prat. It is a solution to the bad design of the primary school's chairs. I was the one in charge of the commercial video, where the product was described and animated.


My contribution:

I had to model the product, given a collection of pictures of an actual prototype. This was an arduous task, because I had to guess the measures and it's always difficult to make it look proportional.


3D model of the product

Once the model was finished, I gave it a rough plastic material, to make it look like the prototype. The node tree for the material is quite simple, I had to apply a bit ofdisplacement to add realism, as otherwise it would look too flat.


Node tree of the basic red plastic material

After that, I just had to animate the model. The rigging was not needed because there was no deformation nor complex movements. The animation was done in about 4 hours. I had to model a dummy and a chair in order to illustrate the product's features. Rendering the animation took about 3 days, that were converted to 6 hours thanks to Sheep-It renderfarm and a friend of mine that has a powerful GPU.


Final version:

After a few corrections made by Marta, the video was done. The day after, the project was presented in front of institutions and bussiness. Here you can see the final version:



Additional work:

I posted a new image in the gallery, that was also an old model, but I resylized a bit to make it look better. I changed the navbar to make it look better in mobile browsers and I also added a contact info section in the about me page.
